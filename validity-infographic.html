Deductive Logic · Validity Test

The Counter-
example Method

A tool for testing whether an argument's structure — not its content — is logically sound.

Ask yourself
Can I imagine a situation where all the premises are true but the conclusion is false?
your answer leads to
✓  Yes, I can
You've found a counterexample — a scenario that breaks the argument. The structure doesn't guarantee the conclusion.
Invalid
✗  No, I can't
Every attempt collapses — making the conclusion false forces at least one premise to become false too.
Valid
Counterexample
A logically coherent scenario in which all the premises are true and the conclusion is false. If one exists, the argument is invalid — no matter how convincing it sounds.
Keep in mind
This is one of several validity tests we'll cover. If this method doesn't click, another will. The goal is to practice looking past an argument's content to evaluate its form — and to understand what deductive validity really means.
